How to read this. Funding settles hourly on both venues; rates shown are the current-interval estimate. Spread APR = (higher − lower hourly funding) × 8760, assuming you hold the pair delta-neutral. You short the higher-funding venue and long the lower one to receive the spread.
Reality check. The APR annualises a single hourly print — funding mean-reverts, so a wide spread rarely lasts. B/E hrs is how long you must hold just to cover round-trip taker fees on both legs; if the spread closes first you net nothing. Overnight / weekend prints (US market closed) are noisier on both oracles and can fabricate spreads. Min OI caps how much size you can run, and the two oracles can drift — that basis risk can swamp the funding you collect.
